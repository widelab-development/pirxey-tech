## What "production-ready" actually means

A backend is production-ready when it answers all these without flinching: How does it behave at 10× traffic? Where do logs land? What wakes oncall? How do you roll back? What's the data retention policy? Who has access to prod? What happens when the DB master dies?

Most freelance backends fail at least three of these. Ours don't.

## How we pick the stack

Boring is good. Node.js for fast iteration with strong ecosystem. Go where performance and concurrency matter. .NET where the team already speaks it. We don't pick tech to look smart — we pick tech we can hand off cleanly.

## Payments

Stripe is our default unless you have a specific reason. We've integrated subscriptions, marketplaces, multi-currency, ACH, SEPA, and disputes flows enough times to know where the dragons live.
