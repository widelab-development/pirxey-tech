## What separates a great front-end from a good one

Most front-end work passes the "looks like Figma" test on day one. The real test is what happens after — when the design system grows, when accessibility audits hit, when the bundle balloons past 1 MB, when the Lighthouse score drops to yellow.

We obsess about these. Our deliverables come with bundle budgets, Lighthouse thresholds, and component documentation by default. Not optional add-ons.

## How we work with designers

We co-own the design system. When we hit an ambiguity in Figma (and we will), we don't guess — we send a screenshot with a question and a proposed answer. Decisions land in Slack, get reflected in tokens, propagate everywhere. No "designer said X but the spec says Y" debates two months later.

## Mobile specifics

For React Native: we use the new architecture, treat platform-specific UX seriously (iOS gestures ≠ Android), and ship to App Store / Play Store with the same care as web releases.
