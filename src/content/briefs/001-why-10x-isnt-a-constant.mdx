  sub="Six measurable forces — each backed by 2025–2026 research. Together they explain the curve you just dragged through."
>
  <div class="factors-grid">
    <ForceCard
      n="01"
      title="Silent duplication inflates the diff"
      stat="+38% redundant logic"
      body="On small projects there is little to duplicate, so AI looks clean. On large codebases it often cannot see what already exists — so it rebuilds the same helper, validator, or fetch wrapper three folders away. Output volume goes up. Real progress does not."
      cite="Pirxey internal benchmark · 53 products · 2024–2026"
    />
    <ForceCard
      n="02"
      title="Duplication compounds context rot"
      stat="2.4× longer fixes"
      body="Duplication was always a tax. With AI it becomes a multiplier: two copies of the same logic mean two stale fragments in context, two reviewers tracing two call graphs, and two places where the next bug fix has to land."
      cite="Pirxey post-mortems · 17 SaaS rescues"
    />
    <ForceCard
      n="03"
      title="AI does not know what it does not know"
      stat="6–11% context coverage"
      body="On real platforms the model loads fragments — a file here, a snippet there — and answers with full confidence. The output reads authoritative, but the relevant constraint often lived in a file the model never opened."
      cite="Pirxey context-coverage audit · Q4 2025"
    />
    <ForceCard
      n="04"
      title="More generated code means more review burden"
      stat="Review grows 1.7× faster"
      body="Volume is cheap to produce and expensive to vet. Every generated block has to be read, traced, and understood before it ships — because one confidently wrong block in a critical path is enough. The bottleneck moves from typing to review."
      cite="Pirxey PR telemetry · 2025"
    />
    <ForceCard
      n="05"
      title="Why seniors on familiar code see ~0 speedup"
      stat="−2% to +4%"
      body="Our working hypothesis: AI excels at isolated, low-context tasks — a new screen, a clean integration, a one-off script. It struggles where senior developers spend the hard hours: cross-module bugs and product logic that require holding the system in your head."
      cite="Pirxey field study · 14 senior engineers"
    />
    <ForceCard
      n="06"
      title="AI is powerful — when the system is designed for it"
      stat="73% decay recoverable"
      body="The answer is not less AI. It is better boundaries. Close context inside isolated modules, even if only at the logic layer. Plan the architecture, brainstorm with AI, challenge its proposals, cut the bad ones, develop the good ones. Soft skills matter: if a developer cannot articulate the need clearly, they will not vibe-code a great product."
      cite="Pirxey delivery playbook · 2024–2026"
    />
  </div>
</Section>
