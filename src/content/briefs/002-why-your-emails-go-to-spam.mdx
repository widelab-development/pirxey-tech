  titleHtml="Four protocols. <em>Four ways to fail silently.</em>"
  subHtml={`Each protocol exists because the previous one wasn't enough. SMTP from 1981 trusts anyone. SPF added a guest list. DKIM added a signature. DMARC added enforcement. BIMI added a face. Skip any one of them and the chain breaks — <em>without bouncing.</em>`}
>
  <div class="four-pillars">
    <ForceCard
      n="01"
      title="SPF — the guest list at the door"
      stat="Layer 1 of 4"
      body={`An owner-controlled TXT record at the apex domain that lists every IP allowed to send mail as you. Receivers check: does the sending IP appear here? If not — spoofed. The catch: SPF allows <strong>at most 10 DNS lookups</strong> during evaluation. Once you exceed that, it silently treats the record as if it didn't exist.`}
      cite="RFC 7208 · in use since 2014"
    />
    <ForceCard
      n="02"
      title="DKIM — the wax seal on the envelope"
      stat="Layer 2 of 4"
      body={`The sending server signs each message with a private key. The matching public key lives at <strong>&lt;selector&gt;._domainkey.&lt;domain&gt;</strong>. Receivers verify the signature byte-by-byte. Tampered in transit? Sent by a server without the key? DKIM fails. Modern keys are 2048-bit RSA.`}
      cite="RFC 6376 · every major ESP signs with DKIM"
    />
    <ForceCard
      n="03"
      title="DMARC — the boss of all bosses"
      stat="Layer 3 of 4"
      body={`If SPF <em>and</em> DKIM both fail — DMARC decides what happens. <strong>p=none</strong> just reports. <strong>p=quarantine</strong> ships failing mail to spam. <strong>p=reject</strong> drops it at the SMTP layer. Add <strong>rua=</strong> to get aggregate XML reports of every server trying to send as you — that's how you discover ongoing spoofing.`}
      cite="RFC 7489 · mandatory for bulk senders since 2024"
    />
    <ForceCard
      n="04"
      title="BIMI — the brand logo in the inbox"
      stat="Layer 4 of 4"
      body={`With DMARC enforcing, you can publish a BIMI record pointing at an SVG Tiny PS logo and a VMC certificate. Gmail, Yahoo, Apple Mail, Fastmail render that logo next to your name. Most setups silently fail on one of three things: the SVG isn't Tiny PS, the certificate isn't from a recognized CA, or <strong>the logo hash in the cert doesn't match the SVG byte-for-byte.</strong>`}
      cite="BIMI Group · DigiCert / Entrust VMC"
    />
  </div>
